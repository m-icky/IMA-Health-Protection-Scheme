About Us

IMA National Health Scheme

Constitution & Aim

Adopted on 27th December in the 75th meeting of Central Council of IMA at Ahmadabad, Gujarat. It came into force in January 2015, with an aim to help IMA members to meet heavy expenses for the management of serious illnesses. In the current health scenario, the cost of investigations and treatment are bound to increase, making this scheme essential for financial security.

To Join The Scheme

IMA members, Spouses, Parents and Children can join the scheme provided they are aged below 80 years at the time of joining. IMA members shall be members and others shall be beneficiary members.

Financial Assistance Provided For

Maximum benefit of ₹3 Lakh in one year

Heart Diseases

Angioplasty, CABG, Valvular surgeries. Upper limit: ₹3 Lakhs

Kidney Diseases

Renal transplantation, Dialysis. Upper limit: ₹3 Lakhs

Malignancies & Tumours

Including Brain tumours. Upper limit: ₹3 Lakhs

Major Surgeries

Spinal Surgery, other major surgeries, Joint replacement surgeries.

Cerebrovascular

Cerebrovascular accidence (strokes & related conditions).

Accidents & Others

Road traffic accidents and other major illnesses requiring hospitalisation.

Reimbursement Rules

  • Diagnosis/treatment costing less than ₹5000 is not covered.
  • Submit original papers, treatment certificate, bills within 60 days of discharge.
  • 75% of total bill amount reimbursed, subject to disease limits.
  • Permissible reimbursement disbursed within 90 days via A/c payee cheque / DD / transfer.
  • No advance payment will be made to members.
Not Covered
  • Charges of special nurse or attendant.
  • Travel, ambulance, food, laundry, telephone.
  • Non-modern medicine treatments.
  • Cosmetic treatments, external appliances (spectacles, hearing aids, etc).
Lock-in Periods
  • Up to 55 yrs: 1 year lock-in period
  • 55 to 70 yrs: 2 years lock-in period
  • 70 to 80 yrs: 3 years lock-in period
